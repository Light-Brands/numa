# Costa Rica Development — Project Overview

## Executive Summary

Large-scale real estate development in Santa Teresa, Costa Rica. 111-hectare property acquired through equity partnership with landowner, subdivided into 20 plots, with phased residential development targeting approximately $39M in gross land sales and $24M in finished product sales on the initial 10-hectare phase.

## Property

- **Location**: Santa Teresa, Costa Rica
- **Total Area**: 111 hectares
- **Estimated Purchase Price**: $5,500,000 USD

## Acquisition Structure

| Component | Amount | Mechanism |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Down Payment | $550,000 (10%) | Cash at closing |
| Remaining Balance | $5,000,000 (90%) | Converted to equity participation for landowner |

### Security Arrangement
If the project does not proceed, the initial 10% down payment is secured by transferring **10 hectares** of the property to the investor group.

## Master Plan Strategy

### Subdivision
- 111 hectares → **20 plots of 5 hectares each** (100 hectares allocated, 11 hectares for roads/common areas/infrastructure)
- Each 5-hectare plot has additional sub-development potential

### Revenue Projections

| Revenue Stream | Estimated Gross |
|----------------|----------------|
| Land plot sales (20 × 5ha) | ~$39,000,000 |
| Finished homes (Phase 1: 40 homes on 10ha) | ~$24,000,000 |
| **Total Potential** | **~$63,000,000** |

### Estimated Profit Margin
**~35%** based on preliminary projections.

## Development Requirements

### Infrastructure & Pre-Development
- Infrastructure and utilities installation
- Engineering and technical studies
- Roads and internal works
- Permitting and regulatory compliance
- Architectural design
- Condominium regime structuring

### Phase 1 Development (10 hectares)
- Corresponds to the land secured through the guarantee structure
- ~40 residential homes
- Projected gross sales: ~$24,000,000

## Seed Capital Structure

**Total Seed Required**: $1,000,000

| Investor Group | Contribution | Structure |
|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| Community Group (10 investors × $50K) | $500,000 | 50% equity |
| Second Investor Group | $500,000 | 50% equity |
| Landowner (land contribution) | $5,000,000 | Equity participation |

## Capital Sources (Jurisdictions)

Investor capital may originate from:
- Europe
- United States
- Mexico
- Other international accounts

This requires multi-jurisdictional compliance consideration.

## Structuring Objectives

1. **Receive cross-border capital** from multiple international investors compliantly
2. **Protect investors** through equity participation, structured instruments, or asset-backed protections
3. **Structure land contribution as equity** in a tax-efficient manner
4. **Segregate risk** — land ownership, development risk, and construction risk appropriately
5. **Optimize corporate and tax structure** under Costa Rican law and international considerations
6. **Minimize tax exposure** through lawful and efficient structuring

## Design Principles

- Lean and efficient structure
- Investor protection aligned with incentives
- Phased development capability
- Scalable from seed through full buildout
- Cross-border compatible
